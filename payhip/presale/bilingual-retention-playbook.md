# Bilingual Retention Playbook (Need-Based Gifting)

## Goal

Increase conversion and repeat purchase without forcing customers to leave reviews.

## Core Rule

Bilingual gift eligibility is based on learner need, not review completion.

## Customer Journey

1. Buy any product tier (L1/L2/L3).
2. Thank-you page asks one CTA: `Choose your support track`.
3. User picks:
- `Standard Track` (English materials only)
- `Bilingual Support Track` (needs Chinese-English vocabulary aid)
4. If bilingual track selected, unlock bilingual vocabulary card gift automatically.
5. Send tier-based upgrade coupon 24 hours later.

## Form Design (30 seconds)

Use a short form with 3 questions:

1. Which support track do you want?
- Standard
- Bilingual support

2. Main challenge now?
- Vocabulary/terminology
- Question interpretation
- Method selection
- Exam timing

3. What do you want next?
- More L1 SubTopic items
- L2 section bundle
- L3 unit bundle

## Reward Logic

- Need-based reward: bilingual card gift (triggered by support track).
- Review-based reward (optional): extra mini bonus sheet or stronger coupon.

## Coupon Ladder

- L1 buyer -> L2 coupon.
- L2 buyer -> L3 coupon.
- L3 buyer -> next-unit or mega-bundle loyalty coupon.
- Bilingual track users -> bilingual enhancement coupon for future bilingual edition.

## Messaging Framework

- Trust line: `You choose your support track. No pressure.`
- Value line: `Start with one SubTopic, upgrade only when ready.`
- Loyalty line: `Returning customers unlock better support and better pricing.`

## KPI Targets

- L1 -> L2 upgrade rate.
- L2 -> L3 upgrade rate.
- Bilingual track opt-in rate.
- Repeat purchase within 30 days.
